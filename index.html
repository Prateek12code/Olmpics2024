About Olmpics:


The 2024 Summer Olympics, held in Paris from July 26 to August 11, was a significant event for several reasons, marking the third time the French capital had hosted the Games after 1900 and 1924. With 32 sports and 329 events, this edition saw the introduction of Breaking (breakdancing), making its Olympic debut, symbolizing the IOC's efforts to modernize the Games and attract younger audiences. Sustainability was a cornerstone of the 2024 Olympics, with Paris striving to make it the most eco-friendly edition ever. An impressive 95% of the venues were either existing or temporary, and the event emphasized reducing carbon emissions and promoting renewable energy. One of the most unique aspects was the marathon route, which traced the steps of women during the French Revolution, symbolizing a message of equality and inclusion. On the competition front, the United States topped the overall medal count, securing 126 medals, including 40 golds, tying with China for the most gold medals. Japan and Australia also delivered strong performances, while the host nation France had an impressive showing with 64 medals. Not only was Paris 2024 celebrated for athletic excellence, but it was also lauded for its focus on environmental sustainability, cultural integration, and modernization of the Olympic Games. The combination of sporting success, innovative environmental strategies, and historical significance made the Paris 2024 Olympics a memorable and groundbreaking edition

Biography of Players

Neeraj Chopra is an Indian track and field athlete, best known for winning India's first-ever Olympic gold medal in men's javelin throw at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. He is also a world champion, having secured gold at the 2023 World Athletics Championships, further cementing his place as one of India's greatest athletes.

Manu Bhaker is an Indian sport shooter who gained international recognition by winning gold at the 2018 ISSF World Cup and the Commonwealth Games. Specializing in 10m air pistol events, she is one of India's top shooting talents and has represented the country at various global competitions, including the Olympics.
